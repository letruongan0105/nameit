Our Purpose

We aim to build an international learning laboratory for young innovators across every stage of the innovation process, creating new solutions to the world’s problems.

To reach that goal, we connect world-class technical, professional, financial, and community resources to anyone who wants to work online or in tech.

Our Story

We saw that creativity has no limits, yet access to learning, exchange, experimentation, feedback, and growth is not equal across communities.

The path into careers and entrepreneurship faces three gaps:

    A signal gap where recruiters and collaborators lack reliable evidence of skills.

    A practice gap where safe spaces to test ideas, make mistakes, and learn through doing are scarce.

    A direction gap where learners are unsure how to turn curiosity into impact or design projects that are both innovative and sustainable.

We chose to build an international community of young innovators.

NameIt is a global innovation laboratory and a community of young innovators that supports them to build their own wild projects, shared resources, and collaboration with others. NameIt helps turn ideas into skills, skills into jobs, and jobs into impact. By equipping people with skills, thinking systems, and up-to-date technology for career growth and venture building, we align our values with SDG 8 and the spirit of innovation.

Our Activities

The Goal

The overall goal of NameIt is to help innovators build their own innovation. People arrive at different stages. Some have not started yet, others have been active for a year or more. Whatever the stage, our goal is to help them become much stronger as a community.

Learning Lab

To understand, you need to learn. The Learning Lab curates videos and learning materials by topics and modules across technology and global issues. It gives innovators an overview of specific topics they care about.

Workspace

After learning, you build. The Workspace gives innovators a place to dive deeper into topics through extended resources, refine their ideas, and move into practical trials.

Challenges

Building brings challenges. Challenges work like a gym for innovators to train thinking and problem solving. This area includes simulation challenges at three levels easy/medium/hard, covering business problems, global issues, and personal product builds. Challenges often come with opportunities. In the same section we attach useful opportunities such as competitions, programs, webinars, and internships.

Guidebooks

Read the instructions before you act. The Guidebooks are practical manuals for innovators. Each one starts with a simple “How to” and walks you through the essentials: why it matters, what steps to take, and where to find reliable resources. You read, apply, reflect, and turn the guidance into outcomes. New guidebooks are added regularly.

Community

Sharing work with a community helps everyone grow. Our Discord community is where innovators discuss new technologies and events, learn together, run peer review, and share achievements and opportunities. We host weekly meetups for hands-on workshops that encourage initiative. We also run Demo Days for pitching and feedback from our mentors.
